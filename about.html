Background

I studied psychology before I found UX, and that did not happen by accident.

People make decisions in ways that do not always match what they say they want. Understanding that gap is what psychology teaches you, and it is exactly what user research is built on. I came to UX from that direction, which means I was already thinking about behavior, motivation, and the distance between intent and action before I learned what a wireframe was.

Since then I have worked across healthcare, health tech, subscription e-commerce, and nonprofits. Green Chef, Ourself Health, Polly Banerjee Counseling, Mangala Shri Bhuti. Different industries, different problems, the same core approach: understand the people first, then decide what to build.

Leadership came with the work. Managing stakeholders, keeping cross-functional teams aligned, and translating between product, engineering, and business goals changed how I design. I stopped thinking about design as something that gets handed off and started thinking about it as something that has to hold up all the way through implementation.

How I Work

Discovery through delivery. AI in the process, not instead of it.

I start every project with research. Not as a box to check, but because designing without it means solving the wrong problem with a lot of confidence. User interviews, competitive audits, card sorting, usability testing: the method depends on what needs to be answered.

I use AI throughout: competitive analysis, content strategy, research synthesis, design validation. Not to replace judgment, but to get to better judgment faster. Claude helped me run an 8-site competitive audit for Polly Banerjee in a fraction of the time it would have taken manually. ChatGPT helped me pressure test data logic during the Ourself Health metric audit. The tools make the work more accurate and leave more time for the parts that need a human in the loop.

I am most useful to teams that need someone who can hold the full picture, from the first research question to the final handoff, and keep the work coherent across all the people it touches along the way.

Outside Work

On a trail, in the garden, or finding the right wine for the problem.

Boulder makes it easy to be outside, and I take that seriously. Hiking is where I think through the problems I have not solved yet. The garden is where I go when I need to stop thinking. And good food and wine with people worth knowing is its own category of important.

I believe the best design work comes from staying connected to people and to things that are not design. The curiosity you bring to a trail or a dinner table is the same curiosity you bring to a user interview.
